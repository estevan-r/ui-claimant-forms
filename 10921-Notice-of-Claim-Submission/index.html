This letter serves as notification that you have submitted a claim for Unemployment Insurance (UI) benefits in Colorado. Please carefully read the following information regarding your UI eligibility requirements, responsibilities in the UI process, how the process works, and the availability of training and education.

Identity Theft/Fraud Notice

If you did not submit an application for benefits and you believe that someone has applied using your name, please submit a fraud report at: CDLE.Colorado.gov/Fraud-Prevention. The web page also includes steps to mitigate risk and safeguard your personal information.

To qualify for benefits, the State requires that you meet certain UI eligibility requirements:

  • You must have earned at least $2,500 in wages during your base period.
  • You must have lost your job through no fault of your own.
  • You must actively be searching for work, be physically and mentally fit to work, be legally authorized to work in the U.S., and be available to accept new job offers.

Your Responsibilities in the UI Process

You must also maintain your eligibility for benefits for as long as you want to receive them.

  • Always Tell the Truth

    Detecting and preventing unemployment insurance fraud is a priority for our agency. You must tell the truth and understand that not doing so is fraud and you could be fined or go to jail. Claims are audited regularly to ensure benefits are paid according to state and federal law.

  • Request Payment/Certify EVERY WEEK

    After you complete your initial claim filing, you can start requesting payment and certifying your eligibility weekly. You need to do this every week, including while you wait for a decision on your claim and during the waiting week. If you fail to request payment/certify for a week, your claim will become inactive. MyUI+ is the easiest way to complete your weekly request.

  • Register with Your Local Workforce Center or ConnectingColroado.com within ONE WEEK of Filing Your Claim

    You must register for work within one week of filing your UI claim. If you do not register within one week, you will be disqualified from receiving benefits until you have met the requirement. If you had a ConnectingColorado account before you filed for unemployment, you still must update/confirm your account details on ConnectingColorado.

  • Actively Look for Work and Keep Track of Your Work-Search Activities

    You are required to keep up with your work search activities and report them to UI while receiving unemployment benefits. We suggest completing 3–5 work-search activities per week. Please report specific information, including the date and type of each activity completed.

  • Respond to Information Requests Promptly

    Please stay alert for information requests from us asking about the details of your claim. Your response will help us finalize the decision on your claim, and it is essential that you respond by the deadline.

  • Report Your Earnings

    Any earnings you receive for work or services must be reported in your weekly certification. You should report gross earnings in the week that you worked, not the week that you received payment. If you do not correctly report your hours and earnings, you may have to pay back some of the benefits you received.

How the UI Claim Process Works

According to Colorado law, we must look at the details of your claim to ensure you are eligible for benefits. The amount of time it takes to process claims varies and may take up to 4 to 5 weeks from the file date.

  • Your claim may have several issues that need to be investigated, such as your wages, the reason for your unemployment, and identity verification, among others.

  • We will contact you and your employer regarding these issues and provide each of you with an opportunity to respond. Please submit your response by the deadline on the questionnaire. If you do not respond, we have to make a decision based on the information we have.

  • You will receive a determination on each of these issues. This determination will inform you of our decision and its impact on whether you will receive benefits.

  • You can appeal any of the determinations on your claim; you must do so within the timeframe stated on the determination letter.

For more information about the UI process, please review the Unemployment Insurance Claimant Guide at: CDLE.Colorado.gov/Unemployment/UI-Claimant-Guide

Contact Us

If you have questions about your claim, please review the information on the contact us page of our website at: CDLE.Colorado.gov/Unemployment/Contact-Us

Training, Education, and Funding Availability

The Colorado Department of Labor & Employment (CDLE) collaborates with local Colorado Workforce Centers to offer free or supplemental support training and educational opportunities through grants and federal funding.

UI claimants may be eligible to continue receiving UI benefits while enrolled in approved training and education programs.

  • Please reach out to your local workforce center to identify approved training programs and to assist you in making education, training, or career decisions. To locate your local workforce center, please visit our website at: CDLE.Colorado.gov/Jobs-Training/Workforce-Centers

  • Funding for education or training programs may be available for claimants receiving unemployment. However, not every training program will allow you to continue receiving unemployment benefits while training. Please contact the institution you would like to attend to learn more.

  • To learn about and apply for Federal student aid, submit the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA®): StudentAid.gov/h/apply-for-aid/fafsa

  • Please keep this letter for your records. This states that you have applied for and may receive unemployment benefits. You may need to provide this letter if you are applying for funding for training programs.

  • Trade Adjustment Assistance (TAA) program

    If you were laid off due to foreign competition or foreign trade, you may qualify for retraining benefits through the Trade Adjustment Assistance (TAA) program. If you think you're eligible for this program, you can apply online at: ApplyTAA.CDLE.org
